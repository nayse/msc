# Camera Application GUI Test Cases

## Test Suite: Primary Camera Functionality Validation

### Test Case 1: Basic Photo Capture
**Objective:** Validate core photo-taking functionality
**Priority:** High

**Test Steps:**
1. Launch camera application
2. Wait for camera preview to load in TextureView bounds [0,630][1440,2550]
3. Verify live camera feed is displaying
4. Tap "take_photo" button at bounds [545,2686][895,3036]
5. Verify photo capture sound/animation
6. Check that photo is saved (storage should decrease from 4.0GB)

**Expected Result:** Photo successfully captured and saved

---

### Test Case 2: Camera Switch Functionality
**Objective:** Test front/rear camera switching
**Priority:** High

**Test Steps:**
1. Start with rear camera active
2. Tap "switch_camera" button at bounds [895,2826][1105,3036]
3. Verify camera preview switches to front camera
4. Take a photo to confirm front camera is working
5. Tap "switch_camera" again to return to rear camera
6. Verify rear camera preview is restored

**Expected Result:** Seamless switching between front and rear cameras

---

### Test Case 3: Video Recording
**Objective:** Validate video recording functionality
**Priority:** High

**Test Steps:**
1. Ensure camera is in photo mode initially
2. Tap "switch_video" button at bounds [335,2826][545,3036]
3. Verify UI changes to video mode (recording indicator appears)
4. Tap main capture button to start recording
5. Record for 5 seconds
6. Tap capture button again to stop recording
7. Verify video is saved and storage is updated

**Expected Result:** Video successfully recorded and saved

---

### Test Case 4: Zoom Control
**Objective:** Test zoom functionality
**Priority:** Medium

**Test Steps:**
1. Locate zoom seekbar at bounds [0,2441][175,2966]
2. Drag zoom slider from bottom to top
3. Verify camera preview zooms in progressively
4. Drag slider back to bottom
5. Verify camera preview zooms out to original level
6. Take photo at different zoom levels to confirm functionality

**Expected Result:** Smooth zoom in/out with preview updating accordingly

---

### Test Case 5: Exposure Controls
**Objective:** Test exposure adjustment features
**Priority:** Medium

**Test Steps:**
1. Tap "exposure" button at bounds [410,144][620,354]
2. Verify exposure adjustment controls appear
3. Adjust exposure settings (increase/decrease)
4. Observe preview brightness changes in real-time
5. Tap "exposure_lock" button at bounds [0,144][210,354]
6. Verify exposure is locked (settings don't auto-adjust)
7. Take photo with locked exposure

**Expected Result:** Exposure adjustments reflect in preview and captured images

## Test Execution Notes

- **Screen Resolution:** Tests designed for 1440x3120 device
- **UI Bounds:** All coordinate-based actions use provided UI element bounds
- **Prerequisites:** Sufficient storage space, camera permissions granted
- **Test Environment:** Physical device recommended for camera hardware testing
- **Performance:** Monitor for any lag or freezing during rapid interactions
